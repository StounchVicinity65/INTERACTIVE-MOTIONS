Why the Scale Factor? ‡¶∏‡ßç‡¶ï‡ßá‡¶≤ ‡¶´‡ßç‡¶Ø‡¶æ‡¶ï‡ßç‡¶ü‡¶∞ ‡¶ï‡ßá‡¶®?

Most screens use pixels to display content, and the physical size of a pixel can vary significantly between devices. Since we're working within a computer screen for visual representation, measuring positions and distances in pixels is logical. To connect these screen pixels to real-world SI units (like meters), a specific scale factor is applied.

Why Frames Instead of Seconds? ‡¶∏‡ßá‡¶ï‡ßá‡¶®‡ßç‡¶°‡ßá‡¶∞ ‡¶™‡¶∞‡¶ø‡¶¨‡¶∞‡ßç‡¶§‡ßá ‡¶´‡ßç‡¶∞‡ßá‡¶Æ ‡¶ï‡ßá‡¶®?

In p5.js and most animation libraries, simulations are updated per frame. Using frames (frame) makes time handling simpler for animation logic. If your simulation runs at 60 frames per second (FPS), then each frame represents 1/60th of a second.
